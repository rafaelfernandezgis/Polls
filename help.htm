Online Help:

The Election Polling Stations application helps citizens locate their election polling station. This application is typically used by citizens during an election season, but can be used throughout the year to determine which elected official represents the precinct they reside in.

Using the Application

The Election Polling Stations application offers a simple way for voters to find their election polling station. When a voter finds their address, the application will select the appropriate polling station and highlight it, along with the respective voting precinct, on the map.

Finding an Area of Interest

There are three primary ways to identify an area of interest. You can interact directly with the map and click on a address when you've located it. You can also use your current location or finally search for an address to find your polling station.

Map Based Navigation

There are several ways to interact with the map:

1. Use the basemap switcher (not available on smartphone) to select the basemap you'd like to interact with
2. Hold down the shift key and drag a box on the map to zoom in
3. Hold Down Ctrl+Shift to zoom out
4. Roll your mouse roller to zoom in/out
5. Drag the zoom slider to zoom in/out

Searching

The search box can be used to find an address:

1. Enter the address you're interested in
2. Click on the item in the list to locate the address on the map

Polling Station

After searching for a respective address, the polling station and voting precinct will be highlighted on the map. At the same time, relevant information will be presented in the Polling Station tab at the bottom of the application. In the Polling Station tab, you'll find:

1. The name of the polling station, its address, the hours of operations and any specific accomodations.
2. Directions to the polling station.
3. Voter registration deadlines and the next election date
4. Contact information

Share Your Map

You can click on the Share Map button to share your map several different ways. You can compose an email, post to your Facebook wall or post a Tweet. When you use the Facebook or Twitter links, you'll be asked to sign in to your respective account.

More Information

The Election Polling Stations application can used by city clerks, county clerks, election commissions or other local government organizations responsible for managing elections to deliver a web-based Election Polling Stations application. This application provides 24x7 access to the organization and typically supplements customer service phone numbers staffed by these organizations.

Browser Compatibility

The Polling Places application is supported on the following browsers: Microsoft IE 7/8/9, Mozilla Firefox, Google Chrome, Apple Safari, iOS Safari 4.0+, and Android Chrome 2.3+.
